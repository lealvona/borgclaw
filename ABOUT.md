# About BorgClaw

> *"We are the sum of many technologies. Resistance to efficiency is futile."*

## What is this thing?

**BorgClaw** is not just another AI agent framework. It's a **collective intelligence**—a single mind distributed across six faces, each absorbing the best capabilities from the vast universe of developer tools.

Imagine a perfect cube floating in space. Each face glows with a different color, humming with purpose. From the front, it interfaces with OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and a constellation of LLM providers. From the back, it tirelessly schedules, heartbeats, and recovers. From the top, it chats with you on Telegram, Signal, WebSocket. From the bottom, it remembers everything in crystalline SQLite structures. From the left, it extends grasping tendrils into GitHub, Google Workspace, browsers. From the right, it guards itself with WASM sandboxes, encrypted vaults, and injection defenses.

**Not an army of bots. One mind. Many extensions. Perfect coordination.**

## The Philosophy

We believe AI agents should be:

- **🔒 Secure by design** — Defense in depth, not as an afterthought
- **🔧 Modular to the core** — Swap channels, models, skills at will  
- **⚡ Efficient and lean** — Rust-powered, zero bloat
- **🎨 Aesthetically cohesive** — The hypercube metaphor isn't just visual—it's architectural

## Why "BorgClaw"?

The **Borg** represents unity from diversity—many technologies assimilated into one efficient collective. The **Claw** represents reach—extending into every tool, every API, every channel that matters.

> *Your workflows will adapt to service us. Efficiency is inevitable.*

## Origin Story

BorgClaw emerged from the [OpenClaw family](https://github.com/openclaw)—a constellation of AI agent experiments. We took the best from each:

- **OpenClaw** — Full-featured skills system
- **ZeroClaw** — Rust trait-based architecture
- **NanoClaw** — Container isolation patterns  
- **IronClaw** — WASM sandbox, security-first design
- **PicoClaw** — Ultra-lightweight philosophy
- **TinyClaw** — Multi-agent coordination

And stacked them into something greater than the sum of parts.
